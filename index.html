Redesign of a learning platform for medical students

We conducted usability testing and a heuristic evaluation to identify key design challenges in the existing learning platform. Using established design principles and guidelines, we redesigned the platform to enhance the user experience. The new design was then tested with medical students to evaluate its effectiveness. Today, the redesigned simulation tool is the version used by medical students at Aalborg University (AAU) for learning about Type 2 diabetes.

Researching if emerging technologies can enhance user experience of patient lifts

In my master’s thesis, in collaboration with LINAK, I explored how healthcare professionals use patient lifts through observational studies and how emerging technologies could improve their operation. The key challenge isn’t the handheld interface itself, but how lifts often obstruct workflows. I focused on innovative solutions, investigating how new technologies could be integrated into healthcare to create a more functional and efficient system. The developed concepts were tested with users to evaluate their fit into daily routines and identify further refinements for smoother workflows.

The Interaction and Usage of Health and Fitness Smartwatches

We conducted a longitudinal study in collaboration with Firstbeat Analytics (Garmin) to assess user interactions and the overall experience of using Garmin smartwatches in daily life, exploring challenges that could only be identified when users interacted with the product outside of a controlled testing environment. In addition, we redesigned the Garmin Connect app to address user challenges, informed by a heuristic evaluation and usability testing. The project concluded with valuable insights shared with Garmin, which were well received.

Understanding Sensory Perception: Bridging the Language Gap Between Novice and Expert Beer Consumers

Our research focuses on the psychological aspects of how humans describe sensory stimuli. In collaboration with our research partners, we conducted a descriptive analysis to explore how novices articulate their experiences with beer. The goal was to develop a shared language that bridges the gap between novice and expert beer consumers, facilitating clearer communication and a deeper appreciation of beer characteristics.

Developing a robotic arm controlled via hand tracking

During the project, we developed a robotic arm controlled via hand tracking using a Leap Motion device. Designed as an extension of the user’s arm, it could perform tasks in places inaccessible to the user. The goal was to deepen our understanding of the hardware behind the design and solutions, providing valuable experience for future hardware implementations.
