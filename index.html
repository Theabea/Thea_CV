PROBLEM

Researching if emerging technologies can enhance user experience of patient lifts

PROBLEM

Patient lifts often disrupt healthcare professionals’ workflows—not due to the handheld interface, but because of their physical and operational integration into daily tasks.

METHODS

Through observational studies, I examined how healthcare professionals use patient lifts and explored how emerging technologies could improve their usability. I developed and tested new concepts with users to assess their fit in real-world routines.

RESULTS

The concepts revealed opportunities to streamline workflows and highlighted key areas for further refinement, contributing to more efficient and user-friendly lift solutions.

The Interaction and Usage of Health and Fitness Smartwatches

PROBLEM

Garmin needed deeper insights into how users interact with smartwatches in real-life settings—beyond what controlled usability tests could reveal.

METHODS

We conducted a longitudinal study in collaboration with Firstbeat Analytics to observe daily smartwatch use. We also performed a heuristic evaluation and usability testing to identify pain points and inform a redesign of the Garmin Connect app.

RESULTS

The redesigned app addressed key user challenges, and the study delivered valuable, well-received insights that helped Garmin better understand real-world user behavior.

Understanding Sensory Perception: Bridging the Language Gap Between Novice and Expert Beer Consumers

PROBLEM

Novice beer consumers often struggle to describe sensory experiences, creating a communication gap between them and experts.

METHODS

In collaboration with research partners, we conducted a descriptive analysis to examine how novices articulate their beer-tasting experiences.

RESULTS

The study contributed to developing a shared sensory language that helps bridge the gap between novices and experts, supporting clearer communication and a deeper appreciation of beer.
